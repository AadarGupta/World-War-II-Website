Canada had also declared war on Germany on September 10th 1939, one week after Britain and France.

During the war in 1940, Canada establishes a Department of Munitions and Supply, so that the production for war material would be managable. During the first World War, the production of weapons was limited to shells, whereas in World War Two, Canada produced a great variety of guns and small arms. They largely produced aircrafts with lancaster bombers, they also build ships, escort vessels, cargo carriers and many military vehicles, consisting of around 815 729 vehicles. Britain got assissted by Canadian an amount of $3 043 000 000. Canada had a uranium mine at Great Bear Lake but did a very limited development with respect to atomic bombing.

The first brigade of Canadian first Division land in France on June 12th 1940, and after a few days the French surrendered to the Nazis.

On December 8th 1941, Hong Kong was attacked by Japanese, when Canadian troops were stationed at Hong Kong, killing 290 Canadian troops, and remaining 1685 troops were taken as prisioners. Out of these prisoners, 260 of these prisoners died before the end of the war.

The Prime Minister of England, Winston Churchill had cited, April 4th 1942 to be the most dangerous moment of the war, because a Royal Canadian Airforce plane had spotted Japanese heading towards Ceylon (Sri Lanka), and gave warning on time for the successful defence.

Out of the 6000 Allied soldiers that had fought at Dieppe Raid, 5000 of them were Canadian. This raid took place in Dieppe, which is located in France on August 19th 1942. This raid was ultimatelty, proved to be a failure, as 1946 of the force were taken as prisoners and 913 Canadians lost their life.

During the Atlantic war May 1943, over 1200 Canadian and Newfoundland merchant seamens had been killed during the period of the war.

Germany surrendered on May 7th 1945, hence the war ended in Europe.

On August 6th 1945, the atomic bombing of Hiroshima occured and a few days later, there was no need to send about 80 000 Canadians to serve in the Pacific, who had volunteered.

Japan finally surrendered on August 14th 1945. The second world war was ultimately over.
